A good pair of leather work boots is an investment — and like any investment, they reward proper care. Follow these steps to keep your Liberty Footwear boots performing at their best for years to come.

## Clean After Every Use

Remove dirt and debris with a stiff brush before it has a chance to dry and damage the leather. For tougher grime, a damp cloth works well. Never soak leather boots in water.

## Condition the Leather Regularly

Leather needs moisture to stay supple and crack-resistant. Apply a quality leather conditioner every 4–6 weeks, or more often if you work in dry conditions. Pay extra attention to flex points like the toe box and ankle area.

## Dry Properly

Never dry boots near direct heat sources — a radiator or campfire will dry out and crack leather quickly. Remove insoles and allow boots to air dry at room temperature. Cedar boot trees help maintain shape and absorb moisture.

## Apply Waterproofing Treatment

Even on our water-resistant models, a regular waterproofing treatment adds an extra layer of protection. Apply after conditioning, and reapply whenever you notice water no longer beading on the surface.

## Resole When Needed

The upper of a Liberty Footwear boot is built to outlast several outsoles. When traction wears down, a quality cobbler can resole your boots — a far better value than buying new.

**A well-maintained pair of Liberty Footwear boots can last a decade or more. Treat them right, and they'll take care of you on the job.**
