A good pair of work boots starts with a proper fit — and proper fit starts with understanding how your feet actually work. At Liberty Footwear, custom fitting isn't an upsell. It's the foundation of everything we do.

## More Than 65% of People Have Mismatched Feet

Here's something most shoe stores won't tell you: over 65 percent of individuals have a left foot and a right foot that are measurably different in size or shape. Yet the vast majority of footwear is sold in matched pairs, in rigid half-size increments, leaving most people compromising on at least one foot.

American shoemakers developed systematic grading methods for shoe lasts — the molds used to shape footwear — in the late 1800s precisely to address this challenge. Modern lasts are built to precision tolerances of .001 inches. The problem isn't the technology. It's whether your retailer is willing to use it.

## Why Generic Sizing Fails

Many consumers end up compromising fit for style or price. The two most common mistakes:

- **Buying too large** to accommodate width issues — the boot fits side to side but the foot slides forward, causing toe drag and instability
- **Buying too tight** and hoping it breaks in — resulting in blisters, pain, and long-term foot damage

Both are avoidable with a proper measurement and fitting session.

## American vs. International Grading

American shoe grading uses arithmetic sizing — consistent increments between each full and half size, and between each width — rather than the geometric scaling used in French or metric systems. This means that when we go up a half size or a full width, the change is predictable and proportional across the entire foot, not just at the toe.

The result: a more precise fit, especially for workers who are on their feet for 8–12 hours a day and can't afford to make compromises.

## What Custom Fitting Actually Looks Like

When you come into our Grand Rapids factory outlet store, we measure both feet individually — length and width — and assess how your foot sits in the boot under load. From there, we have several tools available:

- **Removable footbeds** to fine-tune volume and arch support
- **Boot stretching** for spot adjustments in leather width or toe box
- **Hand-stretching with specialized tools** for more precise modifications
- **Different-sized boots for each foot** — something very few retailers offer, but we can do because we sell factory-direct

That last point matters more than it sounds. Most shoe retailers can't sell you two different sizes from the same pair — their inventory model won't allow it. Because we operate factory-direct, we have the flexibility to fit you the right way, not the convenient way.

## Real-World Results

Custom fitting corrects these problems at the source rather than treating symptoms.
